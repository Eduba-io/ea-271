01 / The Eduba frame

Most organizations automate the wrong things.

About 60 percent of what a team does every day is traditional code and database work. About 30 percent is rule-based logic. Around 10 percent is a real AI problem. The work is sorting those three into the right layers before anything is built.

Applied to a precision-imaging company running AI-assisted reads, the map resolves quickly. Scheduling, DICOM handling, report generation templating, and consumer follow-up communications are database and code work. Routing flags, high-risk finding escalation rules, and consumer-consent state machines are rule-based logic. Preliminary read classification, anomaly highlighting for the radiologist, and plain-language interpretation review are where AI actually earns its keep.

Sorting those layers before anything is automated is the work we would propose to do with the Scan-6 team.
